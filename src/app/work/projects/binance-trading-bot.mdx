## What does it do?

This is a trading bot I built from scratch in Python. It connects to cryptocurrency futures markets, watches price movements in real time, places orders automatically based on a quantitative strategy, and manages risk without any manual input.

The whole system runs on a single process using Python's `asyncio`, which means it can handle multiple trading pairs at the same time without lag. I built it to be production-ready: it has persistent state (survives restarts), extensive logging, Telegram alerts, and 220+ automated tests.

## Key Features

### Real-Time Data via AggTrade Stream
Instead of relying on candle-close data (which updates every few minutes), the bot subscribes to the **AggTrade WebSocket stream** and buckets individual trades into 1-second volume windows. This gives it near-instant detection of unusual market activity, like sudden volume spikes that might signal a big move. The latency from market event to bot reaction is around **100ms**.

### Multi-Timeframe Momentum Filter
The bot watches momentum indicators across two timeframes (5-minute and 15-minute). It uses a latch/unlatch mechanism: when it detects strong momentum in one direction, it "latches" and holds off on closing the position early. When momentum fades (the 15-minute histogram flips), it "unlatches" and lets the exit logic run. This prevents the bot from taking profits too early during strong trends.

The cool part: the latch and unlatch signals come through live WebSocket callbacks, so the bot reacts in under a second rather than waiting for the next poll cycle.

### Anomaly Detection for Black Swan Events
The AggTrade stream also powers a spike detection system. If the bot sees abnormally high volume paired with a sharp price move on a specific exchange listing event, it immediately closes the position to avoid getting caught in a sudden crash. After that, it enters a "recovery mode" where it waits for the market to stabilise (using a WMA(7) downtrend gate), then re-enters with adjusted targets to recover the loss.

### Self-Healing Data Caches
All the indicator caches (moving averages, MACD histograms) are kept fresh through WebSocket callbacks. But if the WebSocket connection drops, the bot notices within seconds (it tracks a freshness timestamp). When data goes stale, it automatically falls back to REST API calls to reseed the cache, then picks back up from WebSocket once it reconnects. No manual restart needed.

## Performance

Each stage of development had a real impact on results. The early builds (stages 0.x) got the system live and trading, but performance was mixed. There was no extra filtering on entries, so the bot was taking trades it probably shouldn't have.

Things improved steadily as the architecture matured. Fixing the async codebase (Stage 1.0) reduced missed signals. Tightening up exits (Stages 1.1 and 1.2) meant the bot stopped leaving money on the table. The biggest single improvement came from adding the MACD momentum filter in Stages 1.5 and the WebSocket cache update after it. That layer added around 20% to cumulative performance on its own, bringing the total to around 28%.

The strategy currently sits at a **Sharpe ratio of 1.2**. The Sharpe ratio measures how much return you're getting relative to the risk taken. A ratio above 1 is generally considered decent, meaning the returns are not just the result of taking on excessive risk.
